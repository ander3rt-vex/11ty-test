# About Commander Nova

## COMMANDER NOVA Deep Space Explorer — Outer Reach Surveying Initiative

Nova did not grow up wanting to be an astronaut. She grew up wanting to know what was out there.
As a child in New Mexico, she would drag her telescope to the roof of her family's adobe house and spend hours mapping constellations that had already been mapped a thousand times before. It was never about discovery for its own sake. It was about the feeling — that particular mix of smallness and electricity that comes from staring into something that has no edge.
She studied planetary geology at the University of Arizona, spent three years doing fieldwork in the Atacama Desert simulating extraterrestrial soil sampling, and was recruited by the Deep Space Division at age 29. Eight years on Mars Base Meridian followed, then two years aboard the ISV Helix conducting stellar surveys of the inner Kepler cluster. She was decorated twice for field improvisation under equipment failure.
When the Outer Reach Surveying Initiative posted a call for a solo long-duration mission beyond the edge of the charted galactic map, Nova applied the same day. She was selected six weeks later.
She is currently 14 months into a projected multi-decade mission. She keeps a photograph of the New Mexico desert taped above her viewport. She talks to ARIA, her navigation AI, more than she expected to. She writes these logs because someone back home should know what it looks like out here.
She does not regret going. She has said this in every log so far, unprompted, as if she knows someone will wonder.
